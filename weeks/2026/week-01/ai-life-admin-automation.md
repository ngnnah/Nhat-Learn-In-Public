# Automating Life Admin: How I Use AI to Bridge Language Barriers and Manage Schedules

I recently booked a gym trial in Japan and received a confirmation email entirely in Japanese. Instead of manually translating and typing details into my calendar, I used AI to automate the process.

---

## 1. The Challenge

I received a confirmation email with critical details buried in the text: specific times, cancellation links, strict "No Cash" payment rules, and rental gear requirements.

## 2. The Prompt

I pasted the raw email text into my AI assistant with this command:

> "Create a full detail .ics file for me to import to my iCal, with all info especially related to fees and usage."

## 3. The Result

The AI generated a code block that I saved as a `.ics` file. When I double-clicked it, it populated my calendar with:

- **Correct Time Zone**: Adjusted automatically
- **Payment Warning**: "NO CASH ACCEPTED" was highlighted in the description (crucial info I might have missed)
- **Gear Requirement**: "Bring socks for rental shoes" was added to the notes
- **Links**: Direct cancellation and coupon URLs were stored in the event details

## 4. The Follow-up (Cultural Context)

I wasn't sure about the shoe rules in the email, so I asked:

> "Do I need indoor shoes? What counts as indoor shoes?"

The AI clarified the gym's policy:

- **Rule**: Street shoes are strictly prohibited
- **Definition**: Athletic shoes with clean soles that have never been worn outside
- **Alternative**: Rental shoes are available, but socks are mandatory
